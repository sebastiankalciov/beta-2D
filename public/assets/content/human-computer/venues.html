Important Journals


• ACM Transactions on Computer-Human Interaction (TOCHI)

The journal covers the software, hardware and human aspects of interaction with computers. Topics include hardware and software architectures; interactive techniques, metaphors, and evaluation; user interface design processes; and users and groups of users.


• International Journal of Human-Computer Studies (IJHCS)

In this journal, research is centered on the interaction between people and computers, emphasizing both the technical and human aspects of the interface.


• Human-Computer Interaction

A multidisciplinary journal defining and reporting on fundamental research in human-computer interaction. It is known for its comprehensive reviews and empirical research studies.


• Interacting with Computers

A journal that addresses the design and use of interactive systems, providing insight into user experience and interface design.


• Computers in Human Behavior

A scholarly journal dedicated to examining the use of computers from a psychological perspective. The journal addresses both the use of computers in psychology, psychiatry and related disciplines as well as the psychological impact of computer use on individuals, groups and society.
