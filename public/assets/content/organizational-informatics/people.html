People


Alan Turing, one of the most famous computer scientists, was a mathematician, logician, and cryptanalyst during World War II.


Grace Hopper an American computer scientist, was instrumental in the development of computer programming languages.She created the first compiler, a program that translates high-level language code into machine-readable code.


John von Neumann a Hungarian-American mathematician and computer scientist, played a crucial role in the development of the electronic digital computer. His groundbreaking work on the architecture of modern computers, including the concept of stored-program computers, influenced the design of most computers today.


Sir Tim Berners-Lee. What would the world be like today without “www.”? Sir Tim Berners-Lee, a British computer scientist, invented the World Wide Web, revolutionizing the way people access and share information.He created the first web browser, web server, and the Hypertext Markup Language (HTML) that enables web pages to be displayed.


Ada Lovelace an English mathematician, is considered the first computer programmer.She worked alongside Charles Babbage on his Analytical Engine, a general-purpose mechanical computer.
